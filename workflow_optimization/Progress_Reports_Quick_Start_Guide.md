# InfoReady Progress Reports: Quick Start Guide
## Practical Implementation for Post-Award Tracking

---

## Why Use Progress Reports?

**Current Gap:** We manage pre-award (posting → selection) but don't track post-award outcomes.

**What Progress Reports Do:**
- Track if nominees received sponsor funding
- Collect success metrics for institutional reporting  
- Identify where we can better support faculty
- Build evidence base for future competitions

**ROI:** 15 minutes setup per competition → institutional data worth thousands in reporting value

---

## Part 1: Fast Setup (30 Minutes)

### Step 1: Enable Progress Reports

**Navigation:**
```
InfoReady → Manage → [Select Competition] → Progress Reports Tab (right sidebar)
```

**Actions:**
1. ✅ Uncheck "Progress Report(s) are not needed"
2. Set Report Title: `[Program Name] FY[Year] Post-Award Report`
3. Set Due Date: 6 months after selection
4. Toggle Instructions: **Yes**

### Step 2: Build Your Form (Choose Template)

**Option A: Simple Funding Check (5 fields, 5 minutes to complete)**
Use for: Most limited submissions, one-time awards

**Section: Funding Outcome**
- Did you submit to sponsor? (Yes/No)
- If Yes: Proposal submission date (Date)
- Current status (Dropdown: Pending/Awarded/Declined)
- If Awarded: Award amount (Currency)
- Additional comments (Text box, optional)

---

**Option B: Comprehensive Tracking (12 fields, 15 minutes to complete)**  
Use for: High-value awards (>$1M), multi-year programs, institutional priorities

**Section 1: Funding Status**
- Have you received funding? (Yes/No) *
- Funding date range (Start/End dates)
- Direct funding amount (Currency)
- Type of support (Grant/Cooperative Agreement/Contract)

**Section 2: Outputs**
- Publications resulting (Number)
- Presentations given (Number)
- Students/postdocs supported (Number)
- Other funding leveraged (Text box)

**Section 3: Support**
- How can RDO support your project? (Text box)
- Permission to share success story (Yes/Yes with approval/No)

*Required field

### Step 3: Add Instructions (Copy/Paste)

```
Dear [Program Name] Awardee,

Please complete this brief 10-minute report to help us track UMD's research impact and identify ways to support you.

Your responses are confidential and reviewed only by Division of Research staff.

Questions? Contact limitedsubmissions@umd.edu

Thank you!
```

### Step 4: Set Reminders

**Automated notifications:**
- 30 days before due date
- 7 days before due date  
- 1 day before due date

Click "Save" → "Publish"

✅ **You're live!**

## Part 2: NSF ART Implementation Example

### Competition Details
- **Award:** $6M over 4 years (cooperative agreement)
- **UMD Limit:** 1 institutional submission
- **Why track:** High value, multi-year, NSF requires annual reports

### Recommended Form Design

**Report Title:** `NSF ART FY2024 Annual Progress Report - Year 1`  
**Due Date:** 6 months post-selection

**Section 1: Sponsor Submission (4 fields)**
1. Did you submit the NSF ART proposal? (Yes/No)
2. Proposal submission date (Date, optional)
3. Current proposal status (Dropdown: Pending/Under Review/Declined/Awarded)
4. If Declined: Will you resubmit? (Yes/No/Unsure)

**Section 2: Award Details (4 fields) - *Conditional: Only if Awarded***
1. Award start date (Date)
2. Award end date (Date)
3. Total award amount (Currency)
4. NSF award number (Text)

**Section 3: Year 1 Progress (4 fields) - *Conditional: Only if Awarded***
1. STRPs initiated (Number)
2. Brief STRP descriptions (Text box, 500 words)
3. ART Ambassadors trained (Number)
4. External partnerships formed (Text box, 200 words)

**Section 4: Compliance (2 fields)**
1. Annual report submitted to NSF? (Yes/No/Not Yet Due)
2. How can RDO support your project? (Text box, optional)

**Total:** 14 fields, ~15 minutes to complete

## Part 3: Daily Operations

### When Enabling Progress Reports

**Before Launch Checklist:**
- [ ] Competition is closed in InfoReady
- [ ] Awardee assigned in system
- [ ] Update Google Sheets: Add "Progress Report Due" date
- [ ] Update Google Calendar: Create reminder event
- [ ] Email awardee with heads-up

**Email Template:**
```
Subject: [Competition Name] - Progress Report Request

Dear Dr. [LastName],

Congratulations again on your selection for [Competition Name]!

To help us track UMD's research impact, we request a brief progress 
report (10-15 minutes) due [Date]. You'll receive an automated email 
from InfoReady with the form link.

Questions? Email limitedsubmissions@umd.edu

Thank you!
```

### When Reports Are Submitted

**Processing Checklist (15 minutes):**
1. [ ] Read submission in InfoReady
2. [ ] Update Google Sheets tracker with outcomes
3. [ ] Export PDF → Save to Shared Drive: `[FY]/[Sponsor]/[Program]/Post-Award Reports/`
4. [ ] If support requested → email supervisor
5. [ ] If success story → request permission to publicize
6. [ ] Send thank-you email to awardee

**Thank You Email:**
```
Subject: Thank You - [Competition Name] Progress Report

Dear Dr. [LastName],

Thank you for completing your progress report!

[IF AWARDED]: Congratulations on securing [$X] from [Sponsor]!

[IF SUPPORT REQUESTED]: I've forwarded your request to my supervisor.

Your feedback helps us improve our processes and demonstrate UMD's 
research impact.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
```

### When Reports Are Overdue (7+ days)

**Follow-Up Email:**
```
Subject: [Competition Name] Progress Report - Gentle Reminder

Dear Dr. [LastName],

Quick reminder about your progress report for [Competition Name] 
(due [Date]). No penalty for late submission - we simply want to 
learn from your experience.

Form link: [URL]

Takes 10-15 minutes. Questions? Let me know!

Thank you,
[Your Name]
```

**Escalation:** After 2 follow-ups → notify supervisor

## Part 5: Prioritization & Scaling

### Which Competitions Should Get Progress Reports?

**START HERE (High Priority):**
- Awards > $1M
- Multi-year programs  
- Institution-building (centers, networks)
- Programs requiring institutional reporting
- Competitions with low historical success rates

**Examples:**
- NSF ART, MURI, ERC, STC
- NIH RM1, DP1
- DOE EFRC
- Keck Foundation

**ADD LATER (Medium Priority):**
- Awards $500K-$1M
- High-visibility programs
- Programs with specific compliance needs

**NOT URGENT (Low Priority):**
- Small awards (<$500K)
- Well-established programs with high success
- Very few nominees (n<3)

### Year 1 Implementation Plan

**Months 1-2:** Pilot with NSF ART
- Set up, test, refine

**Months 3-6:** Expand to 3-5 high-priority programs
- NIH limited submissions
- Large NSF centers
- DOE/DOD major programs

**Months 7-12:** Add medium-priority programs
- Foundation limited submissions
- Internal seed grants

## Part 6: Key Success Metrics

**Track these KPIs:**

1. **Response Rate**
   - Target: 80% within deadline
   - Formula: (# submitted / # invited) × 100

2. **Funding Success Rate**  
   - Target: 50% of nominees receive sponsor funding
   - Formula: (# awarded / # submitted to sponsor) × 100

3. **Total External Funding**
   - Sum of all awards reported
   - Tracks institutional research growth

4. **Time to Submission**
   - Median days from due date
   - Helps optimize reminder timing

**Use data for:**
- Quarterly reports to RDO leadership
- Annual metrics reporting
- Process improvement decisions
- Identifying faculty support needs

## Part 7: Quick Reference

### InfoReady Progress Reports at a Glance

**Access:** Manage → Competition → Progress Reports Tab

**Key Features:**
- Customizable sections and fields
- Drag-and-drop field ordering
- Conditional logic (show fields based on responses)
- Automated email reminders
- Export to CSV/PDF

**Field Types Available:**
- Text field (short answer)
- Text box (long answer)  
- Multiple choice
- Date / Date range
- Number
- Currency
- File upload

### Common Issues & Quick Fixes

**Problem:** Awardee can't find link  
**Fix:** Resend notification manually in Progress Reports tab

**Problem:** Report not showing in admin view  
**Fix:** Verify "Progress Report(s) are not needed" is unchecked

**Problem:** Low response rates  
**Fix:** Shorten form, emphasize "10 minutes", personal follow-up

**Problem:** Incomplete responses  
**Fix:** Too many required fields - make only critical fields required
