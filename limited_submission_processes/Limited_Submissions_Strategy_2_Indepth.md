# Step-by-Step Guide: How to Identify Limited Submissions

## **The 4-Step Framework (Following Hana's Criteria)**

### **Step 1: Initial Screening - Apply Hana's Framework**
Before researching submission limits, eliminate opportunities that don't meet basic criteria:

#### **1A. Audience Check**
- **Question:** Is this directed toward faculty?
- **Eliminate if:** Exclusively for PhD students, graduate students, or non-academic entities
- **Example:** 
  - ⊗ "Dedalus Foundation Dissertation Fellowships" → PhD students only
  - ⊙ "Claude D. Pepper Centers" → Faculty/institutions

#### **1B. Geographic Check**
- **Question:** Are there geographic restrictions that exclude UMD?
- **UMD Location:** Adelphi, Maryland
- **Example:**
  - ⊗ "Parker Foundation" → Limited to Lowell, Massachusetts only
  - ⊙ "NIH grants" → No geographic restrictions

#### **1C. Institutional Eligibility Check**
- **Question:** Can UMD as a research university apply?
- **Example:**
  - ⊗ "DNA CEBR Program" → Requires crime laboratory (UMD doesn't have one)
  - ⊙ "NSF Physics Program" → Research universities eligible

### **Step 2: Deep Research - Find Evidence of Institutional Limits**

#### **2A. Primary Source Research (Most Reliable)**
Search for official solicitation language:

**Web Search Query:** `[Opportunity Name] institutional submission limits`
**Example:** `"Claude D. Pepper Centers" institutional submission limits NIH`

**Look for explicit language like:**
- "Only one application per institution"
- "An institution may submit only one application"
- "Multiple applications from one institution will not be accepted"

#### **2B. University Limited Submission Pages (Highly Reliable)**
Search major research universities' limited submission calendars:

**Web Search Query:** `[Opportunity Name] limited submission [University]`
**Key Universities to Check:**
- Penn State University
- Boston University  
- Vanderbilt University
- Duke University
- Harvard University
- Northwestern University

**Example Search Results:**
```
Penn State Limited Submissions Page:
"RFA-AG-26-006 Artificial Intelligence and Technology Collaboratory"
"Questions concerning the limited submissions process may be submitted to limitedsubs@psu.edu"
```

#### **2C. Grant Mechanism Research**
Certain grant types typically have institutional limits:

**P30 Center Grants (NIH):**
- Environmental Health Sciences Core Centers → Usually limited
- AI Technology Collaboratory → Usually limited  
- Pepper Centers → Explicitly limited

**Foundation Programs:**
- Japan Foundation IPS → Explicitly states limits
- Large institutional awards → Often limited

### **Step 3: Evidence Classification**

#### **Confirmed Evidence (Post as Limited Submission)**
- ◐ **Direct solicitation language** stating institutional limits
- ◐ **Multiple universities** list on limited submission pages
- ◐ **Historical consistency** across funding cycles

**Example - Claude D. Pepper Centers:**
```
Primary Source: RFA-AG-23-017 states "Only one application per institution is allowed"
University Evidence: Multiple limited submission calendars include this program
Historical Evidence: Consistent limits across multiple RFA cycles
CONCLUSION: CONFIRMED Limited Submission
```

#### **Unclear Evidence (Needs Verification)**
- ◑ **Very limited awards** historically but no explicit limits stated
- ◑ **Single source** mentioning limits without confirmation
- ◑ **Forecast stage** opportunities without full solicitation

**Example - Growing Great Ideas:**
```
Evidence: Only 1 award in 2017, 3 awards in 2022
Solicitation: No explicit institutional limits stated
Single Source: NIDA website mentions limited awards
CONCLUSION: UNCLEAR - Needs verification with NIH or peer institutions
```

#### **No Evidence (Standard Application)**
- ◒ **Explicit statement** of no limits
- ◒ **Large number of awards** with no institutional patterns
- ◒ **Individual investigator** programs

**Example - NSF Physics Program:**
```
Primary Source: Solicitation states "There are no restrictions or limits"
Program Type: Individual investigator awards
Award Pattern: Large number of awards annually
CONCLUSION: NO - Standard application process
```

### **Step 4: Cross-Verification & Documentation**

#### **4A. Multiple Source Verification**
Never rely on a single source. Confirm with at least 2-3 sources:

**Claude D. Pepper Centers Example:**
1. **NIH RFA:** "Only one application per institution is allowed"
2. **Penn State:** Lists on limited submissions page
3. **Historical RFAs:** Consistent language across years
4. **Boston University:** Treats as limited submission

#### **4B. Document Evidence Chain**
Record specific evidence for decision audit:

```
Opportunity: RFA-AG-26-025 Claude D. Pepper Centers
Evidence Sources:
- RFA-AG-23-017: "Only one application per institution is allowed"
- RFA-AG-25-020: "An applicant institution may submit only one OAIC application"  
- Multiple university limited submission pages confirm
Decision: CONFIRMED Limited Submission
```

## **Red Flags That Indicate Limited Submissions**

### **Language Triggers**
- "One application per institution"
- "Institution may submit only"
- "Multiple applications will not be accepted"
- University pages asking for "internal competition"

### **Program Characteristics**
- **P30 Center grants** (NIH)
- **Large institutional awards** ($1M+)
- **Very few awards** historically (1-10 per cycle)
- **Foundation institutional programs**

### **University Behavior Patterns**
- Listed on limited submission calendars
- Internal competition processes
- References to institutional coordination

## **Quality Control Checklist**

Before making final determination:

- [ ] Checked at least 2-3 independent sources
- [ ] Found consistent evidence across sources  
- [ ] Distinguished between confirmed, unclear, and no evidence
- [ ] Documented specific evidence and sources
- [ ] Applied Hana's 4-criteria framework
- [ ] Made clear, actionable recommendation

This systematic approach ensures thorough, evidence-based identification of limited submissions while avoiding false positives or missed opportunities.
